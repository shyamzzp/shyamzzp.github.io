The Architect

Dedicated to the
art of dwelling

With an unwavering passion for bungalow design, she brings both technical mastery and an artist's sensitivity to every project she undertakes. Based at the renowned Irani Architects in Colaba, Mumbai — a firm with over 40 years of distinguished practice — her work is defined by a rare combination of rigorous discipline and creative intuition.

Her specialization in bungalows is not accidental: she believes the bungalow to be architecture's most intimate conversation between a building and its occupants. Single-storeyed, grounded, human in scale — a bungalow asks the architect to think about how space is experienced at eye level, how light moves through a room at dawn, how a verandah frames a garden view.

She approaches every commission with the full weight of her dedication — from the first site visit to the final handover. Clients describe her as someone who listens before she draws, who understands that a home is not just a structure but a container for a life.

Areas of Mastery

What she brings to every project

01
Bungalow Design & Planning

A deep specialization in single-family bungalow typologies — from compact urban plots to expansive countryside estates. Mastery in proportion, flow, and the relationship between indoor and outdoor living.

02
Site & Context Analysis

Every bungalow begins with the land. She conducts thorough site studies — solar orientation, wind, topography, views, neighborhood context — to ensure each design grows naturally from its place.

03
Spatial Flow & Vastu

Thoughtful integration of traditional Vastu principles with contemporary spatial planning to create homes that feel both auspicious and deeply comfortable for the families who inhabit them.

04
Materials & Finishes

A refined sensibility for materials — natural stone, exposed brick, timber, handcrafted plaster — chosen not just for aesthetics but for how they age, breathe, and respond to the local climate.

05
Interior–Architecture Integration

The bungalow interior is never an afterthought. She designs built-in joinery, ceiling treatments, threshold details, and lighting as integral parts of the architectural concept — not surface decoration.

06
Project Management

From municipal approvals and structural coordination to contractor supervision and quality inspections — she manages the full lifecycle of each project with meticulous attention and genuine dedication.

"A bungalow is not a reduced form of architecture — it is architecture in its most honest and human expression."

Her design philosophy centers on the conviction that a home should serve the specific life of its inhabitants — not a generic idea of living. She spends considerable time understanding how a family moves through a day before she begins to draw.

This dedication — to truly understanding before designing — is what sets her work apart at Irani Architects. Architecture, she believes, is ultimately an act of care.

How She Works

A process built on dedication

01 — Discovery

Listening First

Every project begins with an extended discovery conversation — understanding not just the brief but the life that will be lived inside the building. How does the family spend its mornings? Where does the evening gather?

02 — Site Study

Reading the Land

Detailed on-site analysis: solar path, prevailing breezes, soil conditions, neighboring structures, views worth framing, and nuisances to screen. The design emerges from the site's own character.

03 — Concept Design

Sketching the Vision

Hand sketches and massing studies that explore multiple spatial ideas before committing to a direction. Clients are invited into this stage — it is a collaboration, not a presentation.

04 — Development

Detailing Every Corner

Working drawings, material specifications, structural coordination, and municipal documentation — handled with the same care as the design. Nothing is left to chance or to others.

05 — Execution

Present on Site

She remains involved through construction — regular site visits, contractor briefings, quality checks. The design intent is protected at every stage, right through to the final handover.
