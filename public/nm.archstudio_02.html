The Architect
Ar. Nikita Mistry
Irani Architects · Colaba, Mumbai

Dedicated to the
art of bungalows

Ar. Nikita Mistry is a Mumbai-based architect with over a decade of focused practice in residential bungalow design. Working at the distinguished firm of Irani Architects in Colaba — one of Mumbai's longest-standing architectural practices — she has built a reputation for design that is both technically rigorous and deeply human.

Her specialization in bungalows is deliberate. She believes the bungalow is architecture at its most intimate: single-storeyed, grounded, human in scale. It demands that the architect think about space as it is experienced at eye level — how light enters a room at dawn, how a verandah frames the garden, how a threshold signals arrival.

She is known for her uncommon dedication — clients consistently remark that she listens before she draws, remains present through construction, and treats every home as if it were her own. This quality of attention is not a style. It is a standard she holds herself to on every single project.

Areas of Mastery

What she brings
to every project

01
Bungalow Design & Planning

Deep specialization in single-family bungalow typologies — from compact urban plots to expansive estates. Mastery in proportion, flow, and the relationship between indoor and outdoor living.

02
Site & Context Analysis

Every bungalow begins with the land. Thorough site studies — solar orientation, wind, topography, views, neighborhood context — ensure each design grows naturally from its place.

03
Spatial Flow & Vastu

Thoughtful integration of Vastu principles with contemporary spatial planning to create homes that feel auspicious and deeply comfortable for the families who inhabit them.

04
Materials & Finishes

A refined sensibility for materials — natural stone, exposed brick, timber, handcrafted plaster — chosen for how they age, breathe, and respond to the local climate over time.

05
Interior–Architecture Integration

Built-in joinery, ceiling treatments, threshold details, and lighting designed as integral parts of the architectural concept — not surface decoration applied at the end.

06
End-to-End Project Management

From municipal approvals and structural coordination to contractor supervision and quality inspections — the full lifecycle managed with meticulous attention and genuine care.

"
A bungalow is not a reduced form of architecture — it is architecture in its most honest and human expression.
Ar. Nikita Mistry

Her design philosophy centers on the conviction that a home should serve the specific life of its inhabitants — not a generic idea of living. She spends considerable time understanding how a family moves through a day before she begins to draw.

This dedication — to truly understanding before designing — is what sets her work apart at Irani Architects. Architecture, she believes, is ultimately an act of care.

Every site visit, every client conversation, every material sample held up to natural light — these are not tasks to be completed. They are the work itself.

How She Works

A process built
on dedication

01
Listening First

Every project begins with an extended discovery conversation — understanding not just the brief but the life that will be lived inside the building. How does the family spend its mornings?

02
Reading the Land

Detailed on-site analysis: solar path, prevailing breezes, soil conditions, neighboring structures, views worth framing. The design emerges from the site's own character.

03
Sketching the Vision

Hand sketches and massing studies exploring multiple spatial ideas before committing to a direction. Clients are invited into this stage — it is a collaboration, not a presentation.

04
Detailing Every Corner

Working drawings, material specs, structural coordination, and municipal documentation — handled with the same care as the design itself. Nothing is left to chance.

05
Present on Site

She remains involved through construction — regular site visits, contractor briefings, quality checks. The design intent is protected at every stage, right through to handover.

The Firm

Irani
